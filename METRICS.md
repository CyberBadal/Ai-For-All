The primary North Star metric for this product is:

North Star Metric

Number of completed audits that convert into consultation-qualified leads.

This metric best represents whether the platform is successfully attracting relevant users, delivering meaningful value through the audit, and generating real business opportunities for Credex. Since the product is primarily a lead-generation tool rather than a daily-use SaaS platform, completed and qualified audits are significantly more meaningful than metrics such as daily active users or page views.

Input Metrics
1. Landing Page → Audit Start Conversion Rate

This measures how effectively the landing page communicates value and motivates visitors to begin the audit process.

Formula:

(Audit Starts / Landing Page Visitors) × 100

A strong conversion rate would indicate that users clearly understand the value proposition and trust the product enough to engage with it.

2. Audit Completion Rate

This metric measures how many users successfully complete the full audit flow after starting it.

Formula:

(Completed Audits / Audit Starts) × 100

A low completion rate may indicate:

poor form UX
too many required fields
unclear recommendations
insufficient perceived value

Improving this metric would likely have the largest impact on total lead generation.

3. Consultation Booking Rate

This measures how many high-savings users convert into potential sales opportunities for Credex.

Formula:

(Consultation Requests / Completed Audits) × 100

This metric is important because the long-term business value of the product comes from converting overspending companies into paying Credex customers.

Initial Product Instrumentation

The first analytics events I would track are:

Landing page visits
CTA button clicks
Audit started
Audit completed
Email submitted
Share link generated
Consultation request submitted

Tracking these events would help identify user drop-off points and improve the conversion funnel over time.

Pivot Decision Metric

If fewer than 5% of completed audits result in either email capture or consultation intent after the first 500 users, I would reconsider:

the recommendation quality
the perceived value of the audit
the onboarding experience
or the target audience positioning

This would indicate that users are not finding enough value in the audit recommendations to continue engaging with the platform.
