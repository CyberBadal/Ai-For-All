Unit Economics & Monetization Strategy
A. Lead Value Estimate
The primary revenue driver is the referral fee from Credex for AI credit optimization.

Average Deal: A scaling startup spends $50,000/year on AI infrastructure.

Credit Value: We help them secure $10,000 in credits/discounts.

Commission: If Credex takes a 20% success fee ($2,000) and shares 50% with this platform, each converted lead is worth $1,000 in gross profit.  

B. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
We focus on high-intent, zero-ad-spend channels to keep the platform lean:

Niche Communities: Targeted outreach in founder-heavy circles (Y Combinator, Kernal, IndieHackers).  

Viral Loops: The "Shareable Audit" feature encourages founders to post their "Savings Score" on X/Twitter, creating organic loops.

SEO/Side-Project Marketing: Ranking for keywords like "reduce OpenAI spend" or "Claude vs ChatGPT for teams."

Estimated CAC: Given the organic nature of these channels, the target CAC is <$15 per audit completion.  

C. Funnel Conversion Math
To validate the model, we track the journey from "Curious Visitor" to "Paying Customer":

Traffic: 5,000 visitors.

Audit Completions (20%): 1,000 users.  

High-Savings Consultation Bookings (8%): 80 founders.  

Credit Purchase/Conversion (25%): 20 closed deals.  

Total Logic: $1,000 (Lead Value) × 20 (Customers) = $20,000 revenue from 5,000 visitors.

D. The Path to $1M ARR
To reach the $1M annual recurring revenue milestone, the platform needs to scale to one of two profiles:

The Volume Play: 1,000 startups per year saving an average of $5,000, generating $1,000 in profit each.  

The Enterprise Play: 250 mid-market companies (Series B+) with heavy API usage, generating $4,000 in commission each through massive infrastructure overhauls.
